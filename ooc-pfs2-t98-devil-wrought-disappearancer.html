AFK: That said, I know that life gets in the way sometimes. If you know in advance that you'll be unable to update, please let the group know in advance. If it's regarding combat, you can either let me know what your preferred tactics are, then I can bot your actions if needed (or just delay after 24 hours of no contact to keep the game going). Mapping: Maps will be done in Google Slides, which is the gold standard for PbP games. My intended goal is that whenever we're in an encounter, when I update the game and if I tag you saying that it's your turn, you have a clear, concise image of a battlemap, relaying all of the information that you'll need to take your turn. Block Initiative: As is the standard for PbP, encounters will be played using block initiative - providing it doesn’t disturb the intended order of operations, allied characters (PCs and enemies alike) may act in any order alongside those who share their same 'block' in the initiative order. Keep in mind that PF2's Delay action has some stipulations regarding ongoing effects, so if you're looking to use it, it'd be best if you familiarise yourself with the rules text first.
1
10:24
Saves: If an effect requires a save, I will roll it in combat to save time. As there's no real player agency when it comes to me saying "The kobold casts a spell on you; roll me a Reflex save, please", I'll just go ahead and do it as part of the spell attack roll to speed things up. If your character has an ability that would influence a check (or you wish to use a Hero Point to reroll a check), then that obviously will alter things. Secret Rolls: PF2 utilizes Secret rolls for a good chunk of skill checks to keep PC and player knowledge separate - - - (I will make any such secret checks in a channel on my own Discord server) - - - so if you wish to know the results after the fact (when it won't affect the gameplay), let me know and I will be happy to reveal any results. Pushing the Narrative: We will play by the Rule of Three: if half the party members agree on a particular action, that will drive the narrative forward. The exception to this rule would be if there is a crucial plot point or important decision - in which case, the entire party should have a say in what happens. This helps keep the game moving forward, as some people like to step up and be the party face, playing more social characters, and some like to keep to the back row and wait until combat to shine.
